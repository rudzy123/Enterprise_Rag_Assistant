# NIST SP 800-61 Rev. 3 – Incident Response Guidance

Source: NIST Special Publication 800-61 Revision 3  
Publisher: National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)  
Status: Public Domain (U.S. Government Work)

## Purpose
This document summarizes key guidance from NIST SP 800-61 Rev. 3 to support
incident response knowledge retrieval and operational awareness.

The guidance aligns incident response activities with the NIST
Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0.

---

## Incident Response Objectives
Incident response activities aim to:
- Reduce the impact of cybersecurity incidents
- Improve detection and response capabilities
- Support business continuity
- Enable continuous improvement

---

## Core Incident Response Functions

### Preparation
Organizations establish governance, policies, roles, training, and tools
required to respond to incidents effectively.

Preparation includes defining incident categories, communication channels,
and escalation paths.

---

### Detection and Analysis
Organizations monitor systems and analyze events to identify potential
incidents.

Detection focuses on timely identification and accurate classification
to enable appropriate response actions.

---

### Containment, Eradication, and Recovery
Once an incident is confirmed:
- Containment limits spread or impact
- Eradication removes the root cause
- Recovery restores normal operations

Actions are prioritized based on risk and business impact.

---

### Post-Incident Activity
After resolution, organizations:
- Document lessons learned
- Update procedures and controls
- Improve detection and response capabilities

Post-incident reviews are essential to maturity.
