# NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 – Selected Security Controls

Source: NIST Special Publication 800-53 Revision 5  
Publisher: National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)  
Status: Public Domain (U.S. Government Work)

## Purpose
This document provides a curated subset of security and privacy controls
from NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 for use in an internal knowledge assistant.
The focus is on commonly referenced controls relevant to enterprise IT
operations and compliance discussions.

This is not a replacement for the full standard.

---

## AC – Access Control

### AC-2 Account Management
Organizations manage information system accounts across their lifecycle,
including creation, modification, disabling, and removal.

Account reviews are performed periodically to ensure accounts remain
appropriate to user roles and responsibilities.

---

### AC-6 Least Privilege
Users are granted the minimum access necessary to perform authorized tasks.

Privileged access is restricted, monitored, and reviewed to reduce the
risk of misuse or compromise.

---

## IA – Identification and Authentication

### IA-5 Authenticator Management
Authenticators (such as passwords, tokens, or certificates) are protected
from unauthorized disclosure and managed through a defined lifecycle.

Compromised or suspected compromised authenticators are replaced promptly.

---

## AU – Audit and Accountability

### AU-6 Audit Record Review, Analysis, and Reporting
Audit logs are reviewed and analyzed to identify inappropriate, unusual,
or suspicious activity.

Organizations define how often logs are reviewed and how findings are
reported to appropriate personnel.

---

## IR – Incident Response

### IR-4 Incident Handling
Organizations establish an incident handling capability that includes:
- Preparation
- Detection and analysis
- Containment, eradication, and recovery
- Post-incident activities

Incident handling procedures are executed in a coordinated and
documented manner.
